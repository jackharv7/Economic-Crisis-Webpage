Great Recession

Time Period: The recession started in December 2007 and ended in June 2009.

Causes:Caused by the subprime mortgage crisis which led to the U.S. Housing bubble. Home permits were much lower than before. Banks offered loans at a lower rate until they sold a lot of bad mortgages than the per capita. In August 2007, the Federal Reserve responded to the mortgage crisis by adding $24 billion in liquidity to the banking system. Federal reserve said agencies can work with lenders to work out arrangement on loans. It was too late though. This led to the collapse of large financial institutions and a $700 billion bailout by the U.S. government.

Effects of The Great Recession:
- After the recession, the unemployment rate peaked at 10 percent, hitting double digits for the first time in 26 years.
- In 2010, The number of home foreclosures in one year hits a peak at 2.9 million properties.
- Poverty increased in the U.S. from 12.5% to 15% in 2010.
- Increase in wealth inequality.


Main events that took place:
- Lehman Brothers file for bankruptcy, It’s the largest bankruptcy case in U.S. history, involving $619 billion in debts.
- For the first time in history, the Fed lowered its benchmark interest rate to zero.
- The “Dow” falls to its Great Recession low of 6,547 points, a drop of more than 50 percent from its all-time high set in October 2007.
- In 2009, Bernie Madoff plead guilty to 11 federal felony accounts.
